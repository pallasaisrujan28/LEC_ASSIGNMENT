# London Export Corporation (LEC) — Company Overview

## About LEC

London Export Corporation (LEC) is a pioneering British trading company founded in 1953. LEC became the first company in the world to trade with China after the country reopened its doors to international commerce. The company was established by Jack Perry Senior following a pivotal delegation to China that forged a groundbreaking agreement with the China Import Export Corporation, an entity under the China Ministry of Foreign Trade.

Over 70 years later, LEC remains a family-run enterprise now in its third generation. Jack Perry Senior founded the company, his son Stephen Perry later took over operations, and in 2006 Jack Perry Junior (grandson, namesake of the founder) joined the business. The Perry family are known as "Icebreakers" for their role in opening trade channels between China and the West during some of the most challenging geopolitical periods.

## History and Milestones

### 1953 — Foundation and First Trade with China
Jack Perry Senior attended the International Economic Conference in Moscow to initiate East-West trade during the Cold War. He met with the Chinese delegation and shortly after established LEC. The company signed its first agreement with the Chinese government in July 1953, making it the first Western company to trade with New China. The initial deals involved the supply of wool tops exported from Yorkshire's textile market. A key early challenge was finding British suppliers willing to accept a Bank of China letter of credit.

### 1971 — Pioneering US-China Trade
LEC was asked to pioneer the first trade deals between the United States and China. Stephen Perry, Jack Senior's son, was dispatched to the US to manage this breakthrough. LEC concluded the first US-China trade deals in late 1971 and early 1972, once again earning the "Icebreaker" reputation.

### 1977 — The LEC-Celanese Partnership
LEC partnered with Celanese to penetrate China's emerging filter cigarette market. At the time, filter adoption in China was only 2%. LEC and Celanese projected a shift to 50% within two decades, but the actual adoption rate soared to 95% over the same period. LEC introduced a production system capable of producing over 256 trillion cigarettes annually, transforming China's cigarette manufacturing capacity and creating a major source of tax revenue. The strategic decision to establish local production facilities from the project's inception was a key factor in this success.

### US Midwest Hub — St. Louis
LEC championed the relocation and creation of a Chinese-US Midwest hub in St. Louis, Missouri. This initiative was endorsed by both Chinese officials and Missouri senators. LEC worked closely with US businesses, particularly in real estate, to lay the foundation for sustainable growth. The St. Louis hub serves as a gateway for bilateral trade and collaboration, fostering investment, innovation, and cultural exchange between China and the American Midwest.

## Core Capabilities

LEC operates on a case-by-case basis, bringing a strategic outlook to each engagement. The company's core strengths include:

- Strategic problem-solving and connecting opportunities across borders
- Long-term thinking and relationship building spanning decades
- Deep expertise in China trade, regulations, and business culture
- Bridging the differences between Western and Eastern business practices
- Joint ventures and partnership creation across multiple sectors

## Sectors and Expertise

Over its 70+ year history, LEC has engaged across a wide range of sectors including:

- Textiles (original trade in wool tops from Yorkshire)
- Tobacco and cigarette manufacturing (LEC-Celanese partnership)
- Entertainment
- Technology and health
- Private equity
- Real estate and economic development
- International trade facilitation and consulting

## The Perry Family Legacy

The Perry family has been at the heart of LEC for three generations:

- **Jack Perry Senior (Founder)**: Established LEC in 1953, led the first Western trade with New China, believed China would re-emerge as a world trade force
- **Stephen Perry**: Joined the company and managed the US-China trade breakthrough in 1971, later ran LEC as Jack Senior moved to chairman
- **Jack Perry Junior**: Joined in 2006, continuing the family tradition into the third generation

The family's philosophy centres on the belief that world trade is a force for good, and that building long-term relationships across cultures creates lasting prosperity for all parties.

## LEC Today

Today, London Export Corporation has completed over 6 cycles of the 12 animals of the Chinese Zodiac (72+ years). The company continues to cultivate opportunities for large-scale companies to work with China, operating with a solid team of experienced minds who understand how to navigate the complexities of East-West business relationships. LEC has had many joint ventures and created many successful businesses throughout its history.
